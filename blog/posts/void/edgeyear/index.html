At the edge of a new year

We’ll gladly let time pass us by until a deadline has been set into place.

Without a care in the world, we live happily ever after.

But as soon as the timers turn red, everyone's asking where all the time went.

My alarm wakes me up at 9 am. I'm supposed to be getting ready for work and heading out by 10.

I'm waking from a dream about me waking up at 3 and realizing I’m late to my shift by 4.

6 hours isn't much. It's just the other 2 or 3 hours that you spend stuck in traffic.

You're technically free, but that doesn't feel like it's the case.

But we keep it up, day after day. We try to make the most out of those remaining couple of hours.

You've gotta eat a meal and wash your face.

It's not Christmas eve, it's a notice that the end of the year is coming around.

It's not Christmas, it's a reminder that time is finite.

31

It's not New Year's eve, it's the moment that you waited for.

The edge of a new year.

In about 5 hours the number ticks up once more. You scratch off another bundle of experience. You promise to change. You ask nice things of fate. You raise your glasses into the sky.

It's the new year.

In about 60 days you're about to forget about it all once again.

And the cycle continues.
