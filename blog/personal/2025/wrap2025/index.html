That's a wrap: 2025.

⇒ Check out the Achievement Log for 2025! ⇐

It is 2026. The new year is here. 2025 has ended.

That feels very weird to say.

It really doesn’t feel like it’s been a year. Though, it sometimes feels like it’s been longer than that... While things feel kind of different, they also feel very much the same. Let’s take it one step at a time.

That was soooo last year… or… last, last year?

I wrote my first “That’s a wrap” for 2024 to get some type of… retrospective look at my timeline and try to make sense of it all. The thing is, that was a very weird year for me. Like every other era in my life, it had its own set of ups and downs, but… the tone was definitely off.

It would be an understatement to say it hit me like a truck (hah) realizing just how many things felt like they were going wrong at the same time… but then started fatefully going right at just the best time as if to try and balance it out.

Some of those sentences, I very vividly remember typing out. The rest? I don’t even know where it was coming from. Still, it feels like it happened only yesterday. But somehow, the things that happened immediately after… I remember as if they’d been moved back a couple of decades.

I think, there were so many things I managed to closed the book on… A few that I never got to close up, and some newer ones that I just happened to open up… I think that’s what makes those moments feel so much more distant.

It’s strange. I don’t understand why time and our perception of it works like that. But it does. That’s why I want to write this post now. To mark yet another “before and after”. To set myself up for success again this coming 2026. Because, believe it or not,

It kind of worked.

So what was 2025 like?

If 2024 felt like an opportunity to turn around my life, 2025 was life looking for an opportunity to turn me around.

It was like a rare return to roots. And a battle to keep up with the pace.

I thought I was the hero of prophecy, but it was all fantasy. I realized I needed to create my own legend. Not just that, live up to it. Live up to the promise I made to myself, but also live up and return to the legacy I left for myself when I was in my teenage years.

Let me simplify.

Up until this point of my life, I’ve either been in one of two states:

1- In a creative trance, where I’m the mastermind with lots of time to spare and create things.

or

2- Disillusioned and working a job just to get by and have something to do.

And even though I have channeled my inner ideas guy at work before, this was the first time that I’d actually spare some of my off days to work on those ideas. Again. This isn’t me saying “ah why bother making all that if i’ll just have to get up for work on monday again” or “i literally don’t have anything else to do so im gonna go and make the next undertale”.

No.

This is me, looking to strike a balance between being busy, and keeping busy.

I have a busy schedule, yes, but that doesn’t mean I’ll give up. I’m still trying. That’s what I’m trying to get at. And it took some getting used to, for sure. The beginning of the year was pretty slow for me, and I kind of disappeared for a while. But once I yearned for control over my life again, I think I found it where I least expected it to be, and that gave me the energy to keep going.

It was fun. It was a good continuation of what happened before. There were challenges, and the way I tackled them led me to learning lifechanging lessons. I’ll get to that now.

The memories we made, the challenges we played, the things that we learned.

I think I can feel the difficulty slider rising, and this is something that will keep on going into 2026.

I’ll start with 3 things that I believe changed the way I live the most.

1 - Having a schedule.

The first and most meaningful change was actually getting that job, of course, but that was 2024… We’re talking about 2025, here. So for the sake of keeping in theme, I will talk about deciding on a schedule.

For a small part at the beginning of the year, I actually had no set schedule. I have no clue how that slipped through the onboarding process, but my supervisors were pretty baffled too. Either way, that meant I was being given very random days and very random times to work. Some weeks it would be 6 days, others it would be 4, randomly it would be 1…. At times I even got 0 days. My paycheck was inconsistent? Sure, but that wasn’t the problem. It was trying to stick to a non-existent routine. When would I have time to do anything? When wouldn’t I? I would have to look at my schedule every week and figure out how I’d allocate my time during that period.

That factor of randomness was the first thing to go. I settled on 3 days. The minimum I had to dedicate to it. Having a predictable schedule meant I could enter a cycle. And it happened. 3 days of work, thinking non-stop about what I’d do with my free time, then counting the days. Day 1, Day 2, Day 3, and Day 4. Day 4 is always tough, but I’ve been getting better at it.

2- Having a goal.

About halfway through the year, my family moved into a new place. There wasn’t really much I had control over in this situation- I didn’t even get to see the place we were moving to until it happened. There was a weird lesson in humility, there, too, but I won’t get into that right now. The main thing is that my new room was slightly smaller, and that an extra 30 minutes had just been tacked onto my commute…

I think about the state of mind I was in at the time, and nothing can describe it better than “defeated”. I think that was the moment where I realized I had no control over my life. Or, at least, I wasn’t exercising it towards achieving the things I wanted to have. It was a tough couple of weeks to just… reflect on things. I’d gone on a… I’d say desperate? shopping spree and bought a bunch of retro consoles I wanted to have… got some upgrades for my computer, some organizing shelves and racks and whatnot… It was also the weekend of a convention. Bought a lot of art.

It struck a nerve. I don’t know what it was. I felt like I had to.

My bank account went to hell that month.

I explored a lot of life paths at the moment. Looked as if I was about to fall off the cliff and my ride had no brakes.

When I came upon the theory that I had done that because I felt as if I had no control, every decision became more conscious. No longer was I fueled by random outbursts of creativity (well, that’s not entirely true. maybe not SOLELY by it, yeah.), but rather by careful planning and trying to have a brighter outlook on things.

It became a coping mechanism, I figure.

It doesn’t really matter what it is. Having a goal and working towards it lends to a sense of control over your time. That goal doesn’t have to be “make a million views on youtube”. It can be things like “I’m gonna clean my room today”, or “I’ll finish this project I started”. What you do doesn’t have to attain life-changing results, it just has to provide some feeling of progress. Some sense of completion. Like we’re not just floating on a river seeing where the flow takes us.

I know what my goals are now. Well. Some of them. The may change, yeah, but whatever, it gives me a sense of direction. That’s what I need the most right now.

3- Celebrating when you can.

Originally, I was thinking of “learning first and foremost” but this is probably more important. Maybe. I’ll find a way to sneak it in.

When I think of the last… whatever, third or quarter of the year, one of the things that pop up to me the most is how much I talked to people about the things I learned (see?), the things I made, the things I achieved… even if they had absolutely no clue what I was talking about.

It’s not even about… having things to talk about with others? Though that’s nice. I’m passionate about the things I’m talking about and I can see a response from someone else. It’s something to relate with, but it’s also a nice way to talk to yourself…

Something I noticed when I look through my previous work is how there’s always something to celebrate. I feel like, every time I accidentally lead myself to my own bandcamp page, I’m compelled to listen to my older music… And I always end up thinking something along the lines of, “Oh wow, It's pretty impressive that I could come up with this.”

Fair. Those are my earlier works, and at that age even knocking on the door before coming in is impressive. But it’s now also happening with stuff from weeks, or months ago. Not just years or half decades away. And I’m convinced that it is a bi-product of a new habit of celebrating what I do more than what I did.

I can’t help but think back to when I told artist friends of mine to post their stuff online, and look at the two differing motives behind it. To me… It’s no longer just about “getting others to see it”. It’s about putting it up where I can see it.

Easily. No friction. No rummaging through files and folders.

That way I can remind myself, “yeah, you did that. be proud.”

So about those memories…

Well, look, I didn’t plan this out ahead of time. I’m half getting things out of my chest, half trying to put a valuable lesson somewhere in here.

So obviously, the bigger life-changing things took priority when writing this.

But it’s not like 2025 was a memory-less year! (It is kind of hard thinking of a definitive memory for the year… But it’s also cooler to remember the year for more than just one thing, right?)

2025 was the year when I:

  • Got a newer car. Finally! A car with electric windows!
  • Brought Gun to life in my own virtual world!
  • Fixed my guitar up at the store and started playing again.
  • Played around with being a made-at-home DJ, and made a bunch of fun remixes.

  • Organized a parody of the game awards: The Tir Awards for April 1st.
  • Went to Super Nintendo World and nearly cried when listening to the theme of Peach’s Castle.
  • My phone RANDOMLY DIED after 5 or so years running it, and upgraded to a fancy new one
  • Visited a cat shelter. Man I even forgot this happened.
  • Moved into a new place. Got the pleasure to rearrange my entire living space.
  • Dyed my hair just in time for the big tournament!
  • Visited an old friend and found some awesome retro goodies.
  • Competed in a fighting game tournament and met online friends!
  • Went through a whole 1-month trial for taekwondo classes. Really.
  • Experienced the 3D effect on a 3DS for the very second time…
  • Learned I hated bowling! damn those things are HEAVY
  • Remade my entire website!
  • Skated for a bit again. Took a fall for the first time ever.

  • Learned an awful lot about old computers. Sometimes I think I learned too much.
  • Got a new laptop! (That’s the one I’m writing this post on- that’s what I got it for :) )

  • Closed the year off with the completion of yet another Christmas Album, effectively turning it into a tradition.

I don’t know what the new year will bring, but I know what I will bring into it.

Screw that “I already messed up, 2027 is my year” thing.

I have no clue what doors will open up for me this year, what incontrollable events will come, what things I’ll come up with, what things others will drag me in,

But it’s time to stop acting like I’m following the script life wrote for me.

We’re gonna make sure 2026 will be a year to remember.

I don’t care what challenges life throws at me, I’m determined to overcome them.

To you, dear reader,

Stop looking for answers in the clouds. Be the one that points at them and decides what you see in them.

Stop looking for people that will fix your problems. Be the one that points at them and decides how you’re gonna solve them.

You already believe in yourself. That’s good.

Start taking the next steps. Start making changes. Change is good.

Start seeing the bridges where you see pits to fall in.

Start building the ladders to help others get out of them.

Be the one who picks the pace,

To turn this world around.
