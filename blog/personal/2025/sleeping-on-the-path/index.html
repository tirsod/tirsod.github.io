Sleeping on the path

Let's see what this one turns out like.

Lately, I've been getting very little sleep.

I don't think I can blame this on anything but myself and my own habits. Though, maybe I should try and find reasons why I might be leaning into those habits and be more charitable towards myself by looking for solutions instead of blames…

Anyway.

Couple of things that keep me up at night:

  • A very interesting show that piqued my interest more than I expected
  • The “tomorrow” and how much worse it gets the longer I stay awake
  • Existential dread

But I think the number one contributor is almost always “the cycle”.

I'm almost always trying to make the most out of those days within “the cycle” that I can use to work on my passions.

And, I think,

Because there's so much to catch up to,

Hardly do I ever go to bed feeling at ease with the results of any given day.

Eh.

Maybe I should switch back to a monospaced font?

Sorry, stray thought.

But I think I'm getting closer.

One thing I've been focusing on: my websites. my blogs. my social media.

I don't spend a lot of time on them. Well. I'm not exactly super fond of social media.

But I'd be lying if I said they weren't important to my craft.

Because, well, they are. Very much so.

They're the gateways to my work.

And my work isn’t perfect, right? But what good does it do for me if I make it perfect and absolutely loveable by everybody and everyone if I don't have clear paths to admiring it?

I want to be a little more organized.

Huh. That's a thought.

Maybe more than one person reading this could identify with that.

Oh yeah,

I guess that includes cleaning my room.

And my sleep schedule.

Yeah.

Who was it that said that “your health was the most important thing every because how are you gonna make art if you're dead?”

Sounds like something a ghost would say.

Well, I'm not going to devote 20 million hours to it- apparently websites aren't even that important.

At least, that's what I think until I hand someone the link and they can't navigate it on their phone.

Maybe I'm missing something?

I already made a post about aesthetics and I went on and on about how they're cool and all.

Manifestation of the soul.

Am I actually achieving this goal?

Well, here's another thought.

Sure, I might be sacrificing my vision by making the very minimalistic site a little more detailed and complex to try and fit more data into a mobile browser.

Right.

But how about,

Fitting THAT mobile browser into the vision? Working with it as an ally instead of as a nuisance?

Surely there's ways to make a vertical scrolling website cool.

So, for now, the priorities are:

  • Finishing the album.
  • Making the websites look and feel good.

More news at 11.
