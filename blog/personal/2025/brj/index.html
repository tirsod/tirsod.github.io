Boss Rush Jam

Well that was fun :)

Ah, January came and went in the blink of an eye. Are we sure it didn't, just, speed up?

Here's the story.

I was invited by a friend to join their team. I've always been reluctant to join these competitions because I'm not very good at time management when it comes to these things.

Or more accurately, I'm not very confident that I can commit to such projects with that tight of a deadline. It doesn't feel right to start something I won't finish, because I've started way too many things already.

But, the prospect was different this time. There was a team, I was mostly making assets and we had a whole month to work with.

Cool! I can get behind this!

The theme is revealed and we're supposed to make a game with spinning in it. Awesome! Let's make an action game and put the characters on a spinning vinyl.

We have a musical sub-theme now, so every boss will have its own musical genre and design. Everyone designs their bosses and... I still have no clue what mine is gonna look like.

I figured I'd make drum and bass because I was listening to some music like it earlier and I already knew how to make it so- That's settled. Maybe he will be a drummer that attacks you with hi-hats and sticks?

But by the time the characters were getting animated I still didn't have a design... By some miracle, I came up with the idea to make a DJ boss. I sketch it up, design the boss fight, and post it in our group chat.

However.

This turned out to be a trap.

One of the reference images I used to draw the boss, was a screenshot of the VirtualDJ software.

"VirtualDJ... I've been here before..."

For practically half a month, all I did was watch DJ videos, make my own mixes, research hardware and learn about the history of DJing from an MIT class on YouTube.

Man,

I could make the best DJ boss fight ever now!

One small problem, though. 7 days remain and my boss still has no presence in the game.

... oops...

During that time we all put our hands together and crunched like we haven't before. We had to try. We MIGHT be able to pull this off. I made my boss' 3D model, animated it, put it in the game, set up the stage, did some basic attacks and had a good time with friends struggling together.

We didn't make it to the deadline... and our game is still unplayable. But... Do I regret it?

Not at all. I learned a lot. And it's not like your work magically vanishes at the end of the day.

A lot of it remains, still. All my music is there. My design is there. It led me into trying out a fun new hobby and gave me a new perspective on many things.

Would you miss out on that... by being afraid of failure?
