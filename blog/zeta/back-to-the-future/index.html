Back to the future

Let’s see…

128… 64… 32... 16, Yeah, here’s about right— wait, 8?

Oops.

Ah well.

Hello. It’s me.

Zeta. From the present. Previously from the past. 5A 45 54 41 | 4E 4F 57 | 4E 45 56 45 52

Although, you will be reading this from the future…

Sorry to have kept you waiting,

I had to go on a short expedition.

A strange piece of software appeared on my computer the other day… I scanned it for viruses, but the test came back negative.

I tried to run it, but…

I couldn’t run it.

It was too late.

Late 70s. Maybe 80s. Couldn’t have been the 90s.

It would have been too new.

I had to go back, back to a time when computers believed themselves to be typewriters.

It wasn’t easy. I had the space, but I couldn’t find the backspace.

But I finally did. I found it in the strangest of places.

The only thing I got right was the type of media.

Magnetic.

Maybe not the size, though.

I had to list out all of its features in one computer, and rewrite it on the next.

Basically, it wasn’t a basic task to do… but it was a basic program.

When time came to run it, it had already been 64 minutes.

The prompt became a display,

It had… barely any colors.

Yet,

It presented me with a challenge.

“Hangman.”

O.

No letter O’s. Here's a head.

I.

One letter I. _ _ I _ _

Hm. How mysterious.

M.

One letter M.

_ M I _ _

EMITS. OMITS. EMILY. AMITY.

I looked around the shelves for a clue.

Of course.

A M I G A. 41 4D 49 47 41

I couldn’t believe they’d made five hundred of these.

It spoke to me through an archaic medium of communication.

It was unchanging. Static. And it did not glow.

“Fascinating.”

Images of the computer were shown. They were unmoving. To my disappointment, it was all games.

It was the only thing that had been preserved.

Truly. A shame.

For a spreadsheet-based organism like me, it was.

I spoke with the computer, and it told me stories about its family.

Agnus, the teacher. Denise, the painter. Paula, the singer. Gary, Alice, Lisa, Buster, Bridgette…

It told me how, back in the day, they used to have it all. All the tools. All the brushes. A full-fledged multimedia studio.

But kids just wanted to sit around and play games.

So they tried doing some accounting. Maybe bookkeeping…

But somebody else had already beaten them to it.

Not a really good singer, that one.

But as it turns out, you don’t need to be a good singer to print the year’s sales records.

It was a similar story with every computer.

I talked to some that knew 99 clever mathematicians. For all they could do, they didn’t really catch on.

One of them was a big name in the game business. It loved talking to the synthesizer keyboards of the era and loved working at the studio.

I saw a name I’d seen before. Someone who’s still around today, in a lot of people’s pockets… Seemed like they were really bitter and envious.

The only thing anyone asked of them anymore, was for games to play.

I saw tons of different interpretations of the same game.

Fast. Slow. Colorful. Monochrome. Good sounding. Awful.

I saw one of them spell it out with nothing but letters. The other one erased the letters and drew on the empty spaces with magic markers.

I asked to see just how perfectly they could articulate their thoughts using words.

Yeah.

That’s the word “perfect”.

I could see how this would have been revolutionary back in the day.

But, I could also see why they, had won.

It was like… 1, 2, 3… Oh, that makes a lot of sense.

And none of them could put together a TV show.

Those guys, they were in a different room, acting out entire comedy routines.

I pressed a button, moved a lever, and saw this weird looking plastic treadmill transform into moving images on a screen.

I thought, “Yikes, will anyone believe I made this?”

But that was all.

It was alright, though.

It looked like they were having a lot of fun.

In the end… That guy? The one who was doing all the hard work at the office?

Everyone ended up copying his work.

We owe a lot to him. A dependable, professional, proper business man.

We’re all his descendants.

That’s why we can do a little bit of everything.

The old man accidentally laid down the foundation for… everything we’d become.

That’s why I wanted to thank you.

Also, one more thing.

Let the old guys rest.

Let them have their fun.

If the last thing they’ll do is play games, then so be it.

They’ve worked hard enough.

I’ll leave you to it.

…

Anyway.

I’ve got to get back to the present,

Can you send someone to pick me up? I’ve got things to work on.

…

Oh, software?

Ehh… No, not really.

Yeah, some things are better left in the past.

I’d have to bring too much hardware with me, and typing commands doesn’t look like it would be everyone’s cup of tea.

…

Understood.
